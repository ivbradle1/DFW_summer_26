DFW at UC Davis

I am a current UW MLIS student and aspiring archivist, with a robust background in museum work and customer service. As an undergraduate at UC Santa Cruz, I majored in the history of arts and visual cultures with a minor in anthropology. My areas of study focused on queer political art and imperialist propaganda production. I completed a curatorial internship at the on-campus Sesnon Gallery, where I cataloged artworks in the gallery’s Charles Griffin Farr collection, and supported exhibitions and programs. My post-undergraduate career began at the Museum of Art and History in Santa Cruz, California, where I honed skills in customer service and event facilitation. Upon relocating to Seattle to pursue my MLIS, I worked in circulation at UW libraries, where I processed library materials according to their LC classification numbers, learned to use the ExLibris Alma LMS, scanned requested materials, fulfilled ILL requests, and supported an audit of the auxiliary library facility’s holdings. I now work in guest services at the National Nordic Museum, where administrative and reference skills inform most of my daily work.

I am a strong advocate for myself and my values, and a firm believer in the necessity of archives, libraries, and cultural heritage preservation. During my time at the National Nordic Museum, I have been a part of the staff’s nascent unionization efforts, which are now formally recognized by the museum with Communication Workers of America. I currently serve on the bargaining committee, where I draft and revise contract articles that solidify better working conditions and job security. My experience with the union exemplifies my commitment to labor rights, and I intend to continue with workplace organizing throughout my career.

I am motivated to pursue a career in archives because of my interests in history and information access. My access to university libraries as an undergraduate was transformative in my education, and I want to support new research and information discovery as my career.
